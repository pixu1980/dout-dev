# Code of Conduct

## Why this exists

dout.dev is a community/open blog: an open space, similar to an open mic, where contributors can propose ideas, experiences, tutorials, opinions, and reflections around the project themes.

Freedom of expression is a core value. At the same time, freedom does not mean absence of responsibility. This Code of Conduct protects people, discussions, and community quality.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- the project GitHub repository
- pull requests, issues, reviews, and commits
- GitHub Discussions related to articles
- comments, threads, and public interactions connected to dout.dev
- communication between contributors, maintainers, and authors when it happens in project context
- content published or proposed for publication on the blog

When someone represents the project publicly, for example through official accounts, posts, events, or community channels, the same standards are expected.

## Community values

We want a community that is:

- open, inclusive, and respectful
- curious, critical, and constructive
- focused on technical quality, without elitism
- welcoming of different points of view
- oriented to shared growth
- safe for people of every background, culture, identity, and experience level

## Expected behavior

Examples of positive behavior:

- use respectful and accessible language
- critique ideas, code, and content without attacking people
- explain the reason behind comments
- accept feedback and change requests collaboratively
- acknowledge mistakes, correct inaccurate information, and credit sources
- respect maintainers' time, boundaries, and responsibilities
- avoid technical or cultural gatekeeping
- help newcomers understand how to contribute

## Unacceptable behavior

Not accepted:

- insults, harassment, intimidation, or threats
- discrimination or degrading language based on ethnicity, skin color, nationality, religion, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, neurodivergence, age, physical appearance, social status, professional experience, or any personal characteristic
- racist, sexist, ableist, homophobic, transphobic, xenophobic, or otherwise dehumanizing content
- personal attacks, dogpiling, flame wars, or repeated provocation
- publishing personal data without consent
- spam, aggressive self-promotion, or irrelevant content
- plagiarism, copyright violations, or unauthorized use of images, code, or text
- voting manipulation, undue pressure on maintainers, or attempts to bypass review process
- using Discussions to continue already moderated or closed conflicts

## Editorial freedom and responsibility

dout.dev is an open mic, not a rule-free megaphone.

Articles can be personal, opinionated, critical, and even provocative, as long as they:

- are relevant to blog topics
- do not violate this Code of Conduct
- do not promote hate, discrimination, or harassment
- clearly distinguish facts, opinions, hypotheses, and personal preferences
- cite sources, references, and inspirations when needed
- respect licenses, copyright, and rights of involved people

An article can be rejected even if well written when it is not relevant to the project or creates risk for community safety.

## Reporting

Reports must be handled with care, confidentiality, and respect.

Recommended channels:

1. private report to a trusted maintainer
2. GitHub Security Advisory, when issue involves security, abuse, or private risk
3. project email to configure: `TODO: community@dout.dev`

Do not use public issues for reports that include personal data, harassment, abuse, vulnerabilities, or sensitive information.

When reporting, include when possible:

- link to discussion, PR, issue, or content
- short description of behavior
- people involved
- screenshots or relevant context
- perceived impact
- urgency level

## Enforcement

Maintainers can take one or more actions:

1. informal reminder
2. request content changes
3. public or private warning
4. close issue, PR, or discussion
5. remove comments or content
6. temporary suspension from participation
7. permanent removal from community
8. revocation of contributor or maintainer role

Severity depends on intent, impact, recurrence, and community risk.

## Safety and inclusion veto

A maintainer can temporarily block publication or merge when there is concrete risk related to:

- Code of Conduct violation
- project or people safety
- personal data or sensitive content
- legal or copyright violation
- damage to the community

The veto must be explained and discussed by maintainers as soon as possible.

## Appeal

A person involved can ask for a decision review.

An appeal must:

- be sent privately
- explain why the decision should be reconsidered
- avoid personal attacks
- include any missing context

A maintainer directly involved in the conflict should not handle the appeal.
