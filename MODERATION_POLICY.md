# Moderation Policy

This policy explains how dout.dev handles moderation, conflicts, and violations.

## Principles

Moderation should be:

- proportional
- documented
- respectful
- safety-oriented
- non-punitive when correction is possible
- firm when people/community need protection

## What can be moderated

Maintainers can moderate:

- comments in Discussions
- issues
- pull requests
- reviews
- proposed articles
- published articles
- project-linked profiles or content when they impact community

## Intervention reasons

Intervention may happen for:

- Code of Conduct violations
- discrimination
- harassment
- spam
- repeated off-topic behavior
- illegal or risky content
- copyright issues
- personal data exposure
- persistent flame behavior
- voting process manipulation
- abuse of project channels

## Intervention levels

### Level 1 - Light reminder

Used for minor or accidental issues.

Examples:

- tone out of line
- mild off-topic
- unclear comment
- poorly phrased critique

### Level 2 - Formal change request

Used when content or behavior must be corrected.

Examples:

- article with problematic passages
- aggressive comment
- missing critical sources
- language needing reformulation

### Level 3 - Warning

Used when behavior breaks rules or repeats.

Examples:

- repeated provocation
- refusal to follow maintainer guidance
- hostile comments
- pressure on reviewers

### Level 4 - Temporary restriction

Used when immediate damage must be stopped.

Examples:

- thread closure
- temporary comment lock
- temporary suspension from review/Discussions
- temporary pause on user's PR

### Level 5 - Exclusion

Used for severe or repeated violations.

Examples:

- harassment
- discrimination
- threats
- doxxing
- permission abuse
- systematic process manipulation
- safety or privacy risk

## Report handling

When report arrives:

1. acknowledge receipt when possible
2. gather context
3. protect personal data
4. involve only necessary maintainers
5. evaluate urgency and risk
6. decide proportional action
7. communicate outcome when appropriate
8. document decision internally

## Privacy

Reports should not be discussed publicly when they involve:

- personal data
- harassment
- sensitive information
- vulnerabilities
- legal elements
- risk to involved people

## Recurrence

Recurrence increases intervention severity.

Previously corrected behavior may lead faster to warning, restriction, or exclusion.

## Moderation of published articles

A published article may be:

- updated
- corrected
- annotated with editorial note
- temporarily hidden
- removed
- archived

Removal is strong measure and should be reasoned.

## Appeal

A person can request review of moderation action.

Appeal should be evaluated by maintainers not directly involved in conflict, when possible.

## Involved maintainers

A maintainer directly involved in conflict should not handle moderation for that case.

If team is small, decision should be carefully documented and, if possible, verified by at least one other maintainer.

## Permission abuse

A maintainer abusing permissions can be suspended or removed.

Examples:

- merging against governance
- unjustified censorship
- using role for personal attacks
- access or disclosure of private information
- vote manipulation
- retaliation against contributors
